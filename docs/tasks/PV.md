# Phase 1: Problem Validation

## Goal

Understand whether motivated students actually need this platform to gain practical project experience and prepare for internships, jobs, and future careers.

---

## Problem Background

Many students work hard in school and maintain strong GPAs, but still struggle to get internships or jobs by their junior or senior year.

One possible reason is that academic performance alone is not always enough. To become competitive in the job market, students often need practical experience that shows they can collaborate, solve problems, communicate, and contribute to real projects.

However, many motivated students do not know what kind of experience they should build, which projects are useful for their target career, or how to start gaining experience before applying for internships.

This creates a gap between students’ academic effort and the practical experience employers expect.

---

## Core Hypothesis

Motivated students are not struggling only because they lack ability.

They are struggling because they often lack:

* Clear guidance on what experience they should build
* Access to beginner-friendly project opportunities
* Teammates who are also serious about growth
* A structured process for completing projects
* Understanding of which experiences are useful for internships or jobs

If students can join beginner-friendly team projects, take clear roles, and follow structured milestones, they may be able to build stronger resumes, portfolios, teamwork skills, and career confidence.

---

## Why This Problem Matters

Not every student needs a project-based platform.

However, for students interested in fields such as computer science, data science, product, consulting, investment banking, startups, marketing, and business strategy, project-based experience can be valuable.

Through group projects, students can learn not only technical or professional skills, but also communication, leadership, responsibility, teamwork, and project management.

These are skills that are difficult to develop only through classes or individual studying.

---

## Personal Insight

This idea also comes from my own experience.

Through leading and managing a company, a student organization of around 30 members, I gained practical experience in management, teamwork, communication, leadership, and organizational development.

That experience helped me understand how valuable real collaboration can be for career development.

It also showed me that students need opportunities where they can practice working with others before entering the workforce.

---

## Current Barriers

Students often struggle to gain this kind of experience because there is no clear platform designed specifically for beginner-friendly project collaboration.

### LinkedIn

LinkedIn can feel too professional or advanced for beginners. Many opportunities require prior experience, making it difficult for students with little experience to take the first step.

### Discord

Discord communities can be useful, but they are often messy, crowded, and unstructured. It can be difficult for beginners to take initiative, find serious teammates, or understand where to start.

### Hackathons

Hackathons provide project experience, but they are often short-term. A few days may not be enough for beginners to deeply develop teamwork, confidence, or long-term project skills.

### Student Clubs

Student clubs can provide experience, but access depends heavily on the school, network, timing, and selectiveness of the organization.

## Third-Party Support

External research also supports this problem.

### 1. Employers value practical skills, not only GPA

NACE defines career readiness through competencies such as communication, critical thinking, leadership, professionalism, teamwork, and technology.

This supports the idea that students need more than academic performance. They also need opportunities to demonstrate practical and transferable skills.

### 2. Employers look for problem-solving and teamwork

According to NACE Job Outlook 2025, nearly 90% of employers look for evidence of problem-solving ability on student resumes, and nearly 80% look for teamwork skills.

This supports the importance of project-based experience, because team projects allow students to show problem-solving, collaboration, and initiative.

### 3. Internship competition is becoming harder

Handshake’s Internship Index 2025 reports that internship postings declined by more than 15% between January 2023 and January 2025, while internship applications increased.

This suggests that students need stronger ways to stand out when applying for internships.

### 4. Project-based learning helps students build workplace skills

Research on capstone and project-based courses shows that team projects can help students gain hands-on experience, practice soft skills, improve employability, and develop communication and teamwork skills.

This supports the idea that structured project experience can help students become more career-ready.

---

## Key Questions to Validate

To validate this problem, we need to interview students and understand their real experiences.

### Student Experience

* Have students tried to gain project, internship, or work-related experience?
* Where did they look for opportunities?
* What stopped them from joining or starting a project?
* Did they feel unqualified or unsure about what they could contribute?

### Career Preparation

* Do students understand what experiences are useful for their target careers?
* Do they know what to do during freshman or sophomore year to prepare for internships?
* Do they feel like they are taking unnecessary detours because they lack guidance?

### Platform Gap

* Why are LinkedIn, Discord, hackathons, or student clubs not enough?
* What support would make students feel more confident?
* Would students use a platform that provides project ideas, team matching, role guidance, and weekly milestones?
