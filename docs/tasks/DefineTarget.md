## Phase 2: Define Target Users

### Goal

Decide who we are building for first and identify which students can benefit the most from a beginner-friendly project matching platform.

---

## Core Idea

This platform should not target all college students.

The main target should be students who are motivated to build their career but do not know how to gain practical experience before applying for internships or jobs.

The platform is most useful for students whose target careers require visible proof of skills, such as projects, portfolios, GitHub repositories, strategy decks, product documents, design case studies, or marketing campaigns.

---

## Primary Target Users

### 1. First-year to junior college students

The main target should be college students from freshman to junior year.
These students are still early enough to build experience before applying for major internships, especially during sophomore and junior year.

#### Why this group matters

* Freshmen need career exploration and early guidance
* Sophomores need their first serious experience before internship applications
* Juniors have the highest urgency because internships often lead to full-time jobs
* Many students start too late and do not know what experience they should build

---

### 2. Students with little or no project experience

These are students who may have strong academic performance, but do not have enough practical experience to show employers.

They may have a good GPA and strong motivation, but still struggle because they lack:

* Team project experience
* Role-specific experience
* Portfolio-ready outcomes
* Leadership or collaboration experience
* Clear evidence of problem-solving ability

---

### 3. Students who could not get internships

This platform is especially helpful for students who applied to internships but could not get one.

Their main problem is often not lack of motivation.
Instead, they may not have enough practical proof that they can contribute to a real team or project.

The platform helps them build “experience before experience.”

---

## Best-Fit Career Areas

This platform is most useful for students targeting careers where project-based experience can directly improve their resume, portfolio, or interview story.

| Career Area                    | Why This Platform Helps                                                                              | Example Project Output                                  |
| ------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Software Engineering           | Students need coding projects, GitHub evidence, and experience building real products                | Web app, mobile app, API, GitHub repo                   |
| Data Science / Data Analytics  | Students need to show data analysis, modeling, dashboards, and business insights                     | Data dashboard, analysis report, ML model               |
| Product Management             | Students need customer research, problem definition, product thinking, and cross-functional teamwork | PRD, roadmap, user research, product prototype          |
| UX / Product Design            | Students need portfolio case studies and design process evidence                                     | Figma prototype, UX case study, usability test          |
| Marketing / Growth             | Students need campaign experience, content, analytics, and measurable outcomes                       | Marketing campaign, landing page, growth experiment     |
| Consulting / Business Strategy | Students need problem-solving, research, analysis, teamwork, and presentation skills                 | Strategy deck, market analysis, business recommendation |
| Startups / Entrepreneurship    | Students need initiative, execution, customer discovery, and multi-role teamwork                     | MVP, pitch deck, customer interview report              |

## Strongest Initial Target Segments

For the MVP, the platform should focus on these three student groups first.

### Segment 1: CS, Data Science, and Engineering Students

#### Target

Freshman to junior students interested in:

* Software engineering
* Data science
* Data analytics
* AI projects
* Technical product roles
* Startup engineering roles

#### Pain Points

* They have class assignments but no real product experience
* They do not have strong GitHub projects
* They do not know what kind of project is impressive
* They struggle to find teammates
* They may know coding basics but not how to build something complete

#### Useful Project Tracks

* Full-stack web app
* Mobile app prototype
* Data dashboard
* AI tool prototype
* API / backend project
* Product engineering project

---

### Segment 2: Business, Economics, and Information Systems Students

#### Target

Freshman to junior students interested in:

* Product management
* Consulting
* Marketing
* Business strategy
* Startups
* Operations
* Growth roles

#### Pain Points

* They are interested in business but lack concrete experience
* They do not know how to show leadership or problem-solving
* They have no strategy deck, market research, or product document
* They want internships but do not know what experience is relevant
* They need teamwork experience beyond class projects

#### Useful Project Tracks

* Product discovery project
* Market entry strategy project
* Business model analysis
* Consulting-style case project
* Go-to-market strategy
* Startup idea validation
* Marketing campaign project

---

### Segment 3: Design, HCI, Psychology, and Communication Students

#### Target

Freshman to junior students interested in:

* UX design
* Product design
* UX research
* Product marketing
* Content strategy
* Human-centered product roles

#### Pain Points

* They need portfolio pieces but do not know how to create them
* They lack real case studies
* They want to work with developers or product people but cannot find teams
* They understand users but do not have polished project outputs
* They need to explain their design process clearly

#### Useful Project Tracks

* UX redesign project
* Figma prototype
* User interview project
* Usability testing project
* Product design case study
* Customer journey mapping

## Lower-Priority Segments for the MVP

Some career paths can still benefit from projects, but they should not be the first focus of the MVP.

### Finance / Investment Banking

Project experience can help students build analytical and presentation skills, but investment banking recruiting often depends heavily on:

* Early recruiting timeline
* Networking
* Technical finance preparation
* School pipeline
* Prior finance experience

Because of this, finance students can be added later, but they should not be the main target at the beginning.

### Research / Academic Roles

Research-focused students may need:

* Lab experience
* Faculty recommendations
* Academic papers
* Research posters
* Graduate-level technical skills

A general beginner-friendly project platform may not be strong enough for this group unless it partners with professors, labs, or research mentors.

---

## Core Use Case

A freshman, sophomore, or junior student joins the platform because they want internship experience but do not know where to start.

They choose a career track such as software engineering, product management, UX design, consulting, marketing, or data analytics.

Then, they join a small beginner-friendly team, take a clear role, follow guided milestones, complete a final project, and turn that experience into a resume bullet, portfolio case study, GitHub project, or presentation deck.

---

## Recommended MVP Target

For the first MVP, the platform should focus on:

**Motivated freshman to junior college students in the U.S. who are interested in tech, product, business, design, marketing, consulting, data, or startups, but lack practical project experience and do not know how to build experience before applying for internships.**
